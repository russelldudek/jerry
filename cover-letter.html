Jerry Hiring Team,

Jerry’s AI advantage is already real: a homegrown system automating a meaningful share of customer interactions at substantial volume. The next product challenge is not to add a cleverer prompt. It is to turn distributed prompt logic into one governed, measurable system for customer behavior - without slowing the teams or damaging the leverage that created the advantage.

That is the mandate I would take on. My candidate thesis is simple: scale the behavior, not the prompt. Each important customer outcome should be defined as a versioned product object that makes the promise, intent, tool authority, human authority, evaluation evidence, latency and cost budget, owner, and release posture explicit. Chat, voice, computer use, back-office automation, and the ChatGPT app can then share a behavioral core while retaining the channel-specific interaction design they require.

My path to this work is unconventional, but directly relevant. Through DudeWorth, I have spent nine-plus years developing AI-readiness systems and agentic workflow patterns using structured context, retrieval, specialized agents, human review, escalation, evaluation, and governance. At Vape-Jet, I connect production, quality, customer success, support, engineering issues, business systems, telemetry, and AI workflows across a company supporting approximately 1,000 fielded robots. Earlier, I built customer-backward operating mechanisms at Amazon and led high-reliability engineering and quality systems at Compunetics.

The through-line is product-system work under operational pressure: expose the real workflow, define decision rights, translate technical tradeoffs, instrument the outcome, and build the learning loop that allows the system to improve without losing trust.

I would begin by mapping Jerry’s six prompt locations to customer intents, owners, tools, handoffs, evaluations, failure classes, and economics. Within 60 days, I would aim to ship one bounded behavior through a shared manifest, evaluation harness, canary and rollback policy, and a scorecard that product, engineering, operations, and leadership can all use. The strongest objection to my candidacy is the absence of a conventional startup PM or forward-deployed engineering title sequence. I would prefer that concern be tested through this work sample and a concrete thin-slice plan rather than answered with title equivalence.

I am currently based in Pittsburgh and actively relocating to the Tampa Bay area. I understand Jerry’s posting limits hiring to specified jurisdictions; I am prepared to establish Florida residency and confirm the timing candidly with recruiting rather than imply that the move is already complete.

Jerry’s opportunity is unusually compelling: a profitable, scaled company with real AI infrastructure, a broad physical-asset vision, direct OpenAI collaboration, and a customer experience where reliable agent behavior can become a durable growth lever. I would welcome the chance to discuss how I would convert that head start into a compounding product platform.

Carpe diem,
