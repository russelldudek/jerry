Jerry’s real mandate is to convert an early, homegrown AI advantage into a versioned customer product platform that can improve quality, automation, and customer satisfaction while controlling latency, cost, operational risk, and ownership complexity.

The real mandate

Own the product system that defines how Jerry’s agents should behave, proves that behavior before release, adapts it across surfaces, and learns from production. This role is simultaneously platform PM, customer-experience architect, AI evaluation leader, and technical translator.

Central contradictions

TensionFailure if mishandledProduct response
Consolidation vs. velocityA platform rewrite slows active teams or preserves fragmentation indefinitely.Start with one high-value thin slice; standardize the behavior object before centralizing every implementation.

The operating model
Agent Behavior Manifest
One customer promise
Many deliberate surfaces

A versioned product object for agent behavior

The manifest is not another prompt repository. It is the minimum product definition required to release a customer-facing behavior responsibly and repeatedly.

Customer promise
Outcome the agent owns, including what it must never imply it completed.
Intent and context
Customer need, journey state, data prerequisites, and activation conditions.
Tool authority
Permitted APIs/actions, preconditions, confirmation rules, retries, and failure behavior.
Human authority
Where licensed experts or operators decide, approve, recover, or assume ownership.
Evaluation suite
Offline cases, edge conditions, shadow evidence, canary thresholds, and production monitors.
Cost / latency budget
Economics and response-time constraints by intent and surface.
Owner / release policy
Accountable owner, version, rollout posture, rollback, and incident response.
Learning loop
How failures become classified evidence, decisions, validated changes, and reusable knowledge.

Release postures, not false precision

Ship

Behavior is covered, economics are understood, authority is explicit, and rollback is ready.

Canary

Promising change with bounded exposure, defined observation, and a fast rollback path.

Hold / human-first

Required evidence, authority, or recovery behavior is missing; the system states limits honestly.

Balanced product scorecard

DimensionDecision questions
Answer and action qualityDid the agent interpret the intent, use the right tools, follow policy, and complete the task truthfully?
Customer outcomeWas the issue resolved, was escalation useful, and did the customer need to contact Jerry again?
Automation integrityWhich automated completions are real, which escalations are avoidable, and where is failure silent?
EconomicsWhat is the latency, model/tool cost, and human-review cost per resolved intent?
Change safetyWhat regression evidence, canary evidence, rollback, and incident learning support the release?
Platform reuseCan another surface adopt the behavior without duplicating policy, evaluation, or ownership logic?
