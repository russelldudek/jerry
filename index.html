The real mandate

Convert a homegrown AI head start into a durable customer product platform.

Jerry already has scale, usage, and automation. The product challenge is to preserve that operational leverage while removing behavior drift, clarifying ownership, and creating a safe runway for the next surfaces.

Consolidation vs. velocity

A platform effort cannot freeze teams that are already serving more than 50,000 monthly chats.

Autonomy vs. human authority

The agent should do more, but licensed judgment and exception handling must remain explicit.

Quality vs. latency and cost

A “smarter” response is not better if it is slow, expensive, or operationally fragile.

Shared standards vs. channel fit

Chat, voice, vision, and computer use need a common behavioral core without pretending they are identical.

Experimentation vs. customer trust

Fast model learning must remain bounded by truthful completion, privacy, policy, and reliable handoff.

New capabilities vs. continuity

Every upgrade needs regression evidence, an owner, a rollout posture, and a rollback path.

Operating model

One governed behavior. Many deliberate surfaces.

Agent Behavior Manifest

A versioned product object that converts a prompt from an isolated string into a customer promise with explicit authority, evidence, economics, ownership, and learning.

Customer promise

What outcome is the agent responsible for, and what must it never imply it completed?

Intent and context

Which customer need, state, data, and journey conditions activate the behavior?

Tool authority

Which APIs or actions can the agent invoke, under what prerequisites, and with what failure behavior?

Human authority

Where does a licensed expert, operator, or reviewer decide, approve, recover, or take ownership?

Evaluation suite

What offline, shadow, canary, and production evidence proves quality before and after release?

Cost and latency budget

What economics and response-time constraints define a useful customer experience at scale?

Owner and release policy

Who owns the behavior, its adapters, the release posture, rollback, and post-launch learning?

Product scorecard

Measure the whole behavior, not just the answer.

The platform earns trust when product, engineering, operations, and leadership can see the same tradeoffs and make explicit release decisions.

Answer and action quality

Task success, factual correctness, policy adherence, tool success, and customer resolution.

Customer outcome

CSAT, completion, repeat contact, escalation quality, and recovery from exceptions.

Automation integrity

Automation rate with false-completion, unnecessary escalation, and silent-failure controls.

Economics

Latency, model and tool cost per resolved intent, and the operational cost of human review.

Change safety

Evaluation coverage, canary evidence, rollback rate, regression escape, and incident learning.

Platform reuse

Behaviors adopted across surfaces without duplicating policy, evaluation, or ownership logic.

Human leverage

Licensed and operational expertise applied to high-value judgment rather than preventable rework.

Learning velocity

Time from observed failure to classified evidence, product decision, validated change, and release.

First 90 days

Map the behavior. Prove the thin slice. Create the compounding loop.

Days 0-30

See the real system

  • Inventory all six prompt locations, behavior owners, customer intents, tool calls, handoffs, evaluations, and failure classes.
  • Baseline quality, CSAT, automation, escalation, latency, and cost by intent rather than one blended average.
  • Trace one high-volume journey end-to-end with product, engineering, operations, and licensed expertise.
  • Confirm Tampa Bay relocation timing and operating availability.
Days 31-60

Install the manifest

  • Define the minimum Agent Behavior Manifest and versioning policy.
  • Create an evaluation and release path for one bounded customer journey.
  • Clarify human authority, exception recovery, canary rules, and rollback.
  • Ship one thin-slice improvement with a shared scorecard and post-release learning review.
Days 61-90

Turn proof into platform

  • Separate reusable behavioral core from surface-specific adapters.
  • Prioritize the next roadmap bets using customer value, readiness, risk, economics, and reusable learning.
  • Apply the mechanism to a voice, ChatGPT, or computer-use candidate workflow.
  • Establish a durable operating cadence for product standards, exceptions, and learning.
Hard objection

“He has not followed a conventional startup PM or forward-deployed engineering title path.”

That is a legitimate screening concern. It should be tested through the work, not argued away with title equivalence.

I would ask Jerry to evaluate whether I can define an agent behavior, trace its dependencies, write the evaluation and release logic, interrogate the data, make the tradeoffs legible, and align engineering with the operating teams who inherit the outcome.

My de-risking mechanism is a 30-day evidence map and one 60-day thin slice with explicit quality, economics, human-authority, and rollback criteria.

  • Can I turn one customer intent into a complete behavior manifest?
  • Can I identify where automation creates false confidence or unnecessary handoff?
  • Can I write a useful SQL question and interpret the operational evidence?
  • Can I make latency, cost, quality, and customer risk tradeoffs explicit?
  • Can I translate technical constraints for executives and operational reality for engineers?
Executive interview questions

Questions designed to discover the mandate, not perform homework theater.

01Where does Jerry currently feel the cost of fragmented prompt ownership most sharply: customer inconsistency, release risk, engineering rework, economics, or slower roadmap decisions?
02Which automation metric is most likely to look healthy while hiding a customer or operational failure mode?
03What customer journey would be the best proving ground for a unified behavior standard without turning the platform effort into a rewrite?
04How are licensed-agent authority, escalation context, and post-handoff learning represented in the current product system?
05What must be true for voice or computer use to become a roadmap commitment rather than a model-capability experiment?
06When the COO says this growth lever is working, which customer, economic, and organizational evidence will have changed?
